## Reading the Organization

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Power Dynamics" icon="bolt">
    **Look for these signals:**

    - Who speaks on earnings calls
    - Who announces major initiatives
    - Who has the most direct reports
    - Who owns P&L responsibility
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Decision Making" icon="gavel">
    **Common patterns:**

    - Centralized: Everything goes through HQ
    - Decentralized: Divisions have autonomy
    - Matrix: Multiple approvals needed
    - Agile: Small teams with fast decisions
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Budget Control" icon="wallet">
    **Follow the money:**

    - Operating budget holders
    - Capital expenditure approvers
    - Innovation fund controllers
    - Discretionary spend limits
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Sales Strategy by Structure Type

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Hierarchical Organizations" icon="building">
    - Start at the top
    - Get executive sponsorship
    - Work down through layers
    - Respect chain of command
  </Card>
  <Card title="Flat Organizations" icon="users">
    - Build broad consensus
    - Focus on influencers
    - Demonstrate team value
    - Enable peer champions
  </Card>
  <Card title="Matrix Organizations" icon="network-wired">
    - Map all stakeholders
    - Align competing interests
    - Build coalition support
    - Navigate carefully
  </Card>
  <Card title="Divisional Organizations" icon="building-columns">
    - Land and expand
    - Prove value in one division
    - Use internal references
    - Scale horizontally
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Tip>
  **Multi-threading hack:** Always ask "Who else would need to be involved in this decision?" to uncover hidden stakeholders
