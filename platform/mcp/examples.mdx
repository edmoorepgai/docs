Once PG:AI is connected to your AI tool, you interact with it using natural language. Here are real-world examples of how sellers, BDRs, and sales leaders use PG:AI through Claude, Cursor, and other AI assistants.

## Meeting Preparation

The most common use case. Ask your AI to prepare you for an upcoming meeting, and it pulls together everything PG:AI knows about the account.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Simple Prep">
    **Prompt:**
    > I have a meeting with Airtable tomorrow. Help me prepare.

    **What happens:** Your AI calls `pgai_company` with `include: ["profile", "insights", "financials", "technologies", "contacts_summary", "events"]`, then synthesises everything into a briefing.

    **You get:** Company overview, recent strategic insights, financial highlights, technology stack, key contacts, and any recent events  -  all in one response.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Focused Prep">
    **Prompt:**
    > Generate a meeting prep pack for my call with the CTO of Harness. The topic is CI/CD pipeline efficiency.

    **What happens:** Your AI calls `pgai_generate` with `type: "meeting_prep"` and `topic: "CI/CD pipeline efficiency"`, producing a structured prep pack.

    **You get:** A comprehensive preparation document with talking points, discovery questions, and strategic angles  -  all tailored to Harness and the CTO's likely priorities.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Contact Discovery

Find and research the right people at your target accounts.

**Find contacts by role:**
> *"Search for VP of Engineering contacts at my tracked accounts"*

**Research a specific person:**
> *"Find Sarah Chen at Airtable and get her full contact details"*

**Discover new contacts:**
> *"Research the top 10 data and analytics contacts at Harness"*

**Save a contact you found externally:**
> *"Save this contact: James Wilson, Head of Data Engineering at Tesco, james.wilson@tesco.com, linkedin.com/in/jameswilson"*

<Tip>
  Contact discovery works best as a two-step conversation. First search for contacts, then ask for full details on the ones you're interested in.
</Tip>

## Content Generation

Let PG:AI generate personalised, intelligence-backed sales content.

### Emails

> *"Write a professional email to the VP of Data at Frasers Group about our analytics platform. Focus on their recent cloud migration."*

Your AI calls `pgai_generate` with `type: "email"`, pulling in the latest insights about Frasers Group to personalise the message.

### Email Sequences

> *"Create a 4-step email sequence for Airtable focused on data governance. Keep the tone consultative."*

Generates a multi-touch sequence where each step builds on PG:AI's intelligence about the company.

### Call Scripts

> *"Build a call script for reaching out to the CTO at Harness about observability"*

Produces a structured script with an opening, discovery questions, value propositions, and objection handling  -  all tailored to Harness.

## Advanced Workflows

### Value Pyramids

> *"Generate a value pyramid for Snowflake  -  focus on data engineering and cost optimisation"*

Creates a structured value proposition framework showing how your solution maps to the company's priorities at executive, management, and operational levels.

### Enablement Guides

> *"Create an enablement guide for the Tesco account focused on their digital transformation initiatives"*

Produces a comprehensive guide your team can use to ramp up on the account.

### Multi-Step Research

Have a natural conversation that chains multiple tools together:

> **You:** *"What companies am I tracking in the EMEA territory?"*
>
> **AI:** Lists your EMEA companies from the territories resource.
>
> **You:** *"Which of those are hiring for data roles?"*
>
> **AI:** Searches job postings across those companies.
>
> **You:** *"Great  -  generate meeting prep for the top 3 that are actively hiring"*
>
> **AI:** Creates tailored prep packs for each company.

## Tips for Better Results

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Be specific about what you need">
    Instead of *"Tell me about Airtable"*, try *"What are Airtable's latest strategic insights and what technologies are they using?"*  -  this helps the AI request exactly the right data sections.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Use company names naturally">
    PG:AI fuzzy-matches company names, so *"Airtable"*, *"airtable"*, and *"Airtable Inc"* all work. Use whichever feels natural.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Chain requests in conversation">
    Your AI remembers context within a conversation. Start with research, then ask for content generation based on what you learned  -  the AI will connect the dots.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Set the tone for generated content">
    When generating emails or call scripts, specify the tone: *professional*, *casual*, *formal*, or *consultative*. If you don't specify, PG:AI uses sensible defaults.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Filter jobs with keywords">
    When looking at job postings, include a keyword: *"Show me Airtable's job postings that mention machine learning"*  -  this filters results before they reach you.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
